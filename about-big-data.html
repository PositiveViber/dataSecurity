Latest Research on Smart Home Security

Smart homes are becoming increasingly common, and with them, concerns about privacy and security are growing. Cutting-edge research has revealed that these interconnected havens are not as secure as we might hope. IoT devices, from our phones to smart TVs and even baby monitors, can inadvertently expose personal information like location data or device specifics through local networks. These findings are crucial in pushing for more stringent security measures in smart home devices.

PrivacyCon 2024 Insights

PrivacyCon 2024 by the FTC explored critical challenges in consumer privacy and data security. Experts from various sectors discussed the latest research on automated systems, health privacy, and online surveillance. Key topics included the privacy of children and teens, the impact of deepfakes, and the pervasive nature of worker surveillance.
